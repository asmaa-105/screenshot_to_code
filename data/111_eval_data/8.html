[Notice] Change of payment currencies in 5 countries and temporary suspension of paid purchases 2024-05-20

Hello! This is the Galaxy Store Seller Portal. Thank you for always using the Seller Portal and for your continued interest. To reduce fluctuations in product prices due to some exchange rate changes and preserve the profits of sellers, we will be changing the payment currency for the Galaxy Store in five countries: Romania, Bahrain, Kuwait, Tunisia, and Yemen. For this, Galaxy Store/Galaxy Themes/Samsung IAP-applied in-app paid purchases will be suspended from 00:00 to 08:00 on June 10, 2024 (GMT+0, subject to change). All features except paid purchases may be used as usual. - Date and time of currency change: Date Target countries June 10, 2024 00:00 to 08:00 (GMT+0) 1) Romania: Euro (EUR) → Romanian New Leu (RON) 2) Yemen: Rial (YER) → US Dollar (USD) June 11, 2024 00:00 to 08:00 (GMT+0) 1) Bahrain: Dinar (BHD) → US Dollar (USD) 2) Kuwait: Dinar (KWD) → US Dollar (USD) 3) Tunisia: Dinar (TND) → US Dollar (USD) - Details: Changes to the currency of paid products and items. - Precautions: “Price” changes restricted, existing subscriptions canceled * If a seller changes the price of a product/item during the time indicated above, the changed price of the product/item may not be reflected correctly. * For any products/items with a lower or higher price than the existing standard price by using a lower/higher price from the existing price, please make the product price change after this service change work is completed. * To prevent disadvantages to users due to currency changes, existing subscriptions will be canceled. We ask sellers to remind users to re-subscribe. Thank you.

[Notice] Galaxy Store Sales Tax Remittance 2024-03-28

Galaxy Store Sales Tax Remittance For any purchase made in Galaxy Store, Galaxy Store automatically collects the sales tax or value added tax (VAT) from the user according to the tax laws of each country. In this notice, “sales tax” is used to refer to both the sales tax and VAT. Depending on the business model of a country, either the seller or Galaxy Store must remit the sales tax to the appropriate tax authority. This notice provides a brief description of the two business models (agent and commissionaire) and a list of agent countries where there is an exception to how the tax is remitted. [Agent business model] The seller is responsible for sales tax remittance - The seller is the merchant on record. - Galaxy Store acts as an intermediary between the seller and user. The seller's content is sold directly to the user. - Galaxy Store collects the sales tax from users. - The collected sales tax is included in the payment to the seller. Galaxy Store only receives brokerage fees. However, in some agent countries, the collected sales tax is not included in the payment to the seller because Galaxy Store must remit the sales tax on their behalf (see “Exceptions to the agent business model” below). - The seller declares and remits the sales tax (except where Galaxy Store must remit the sales tax on the seller’s behalf, see “Exceptions to the agent business model” below). - In the settlement report spreadsheet, the sales tax remitted by Galaxy Store is displayed in column O (USD) and column T (KRW). For most agent countries, this will be 0 (zero). In some agent countries, Galaxy Store must remit the sales tax on the seller’s behalf (see “Exceptions to the agent business model” below). [Commissionaire business model] Galaxy Store is responsible for sales tax remittance - Galaxy Store is the merchant on record. - Galaxy Store acts as a consignment agency. Galaxy Store buys and sells the seller's content. - Galaxy Store collects the sales tax from users. - The collected sales tax is not included in the payment to the seller. - Galaxy Store declares and remits the sales tax. - In the settlement report spreadsheet, the sales tax remitted by Galaxy Store is displayed in column O (USD) and column T (KRW). For a list of countries and their associated business model, refer to Article 20, Paragraph 1 of the Terms and Conditions. Exceptions to the agent business model In certain agent countries, an intermediary (Galaxy Store) must remit the sales tax on the seller's behalf. For these countries, the collected sales tax is not included in the payment to the seller. Instead, the collected sales tax is remitted to the appropriate tax authority. In the settlement report spreadsheet, the sales tax remitted by Galaxy Store is displayed in column O (USD) and column T (KRW). [Notice] Notice on conversion to free store (6 countries including Armenia and Ghana) 2024-02-22

A message from the Galaxy Store Seller Portal. From March 4, 2024, sales of paid content (paid apps, IAP content, etc.) will be discontinued in the six countries below. • Armenia • Ghana • Kenya • Kyrgyzstan • Nigeria • Tanzania From March 4, 2024, any paid content distribution will be systematically processed to ensure that it is not distributed in these countries. However, free content can still be distributed in these countries. If you have any questions, please contact us through Seller Portal > Customer Support > Contact Us. FAQ Q. Can I receive full compensation for profits generated in these countries? A. Yes. Payments will be made on a regular schedule. Q. Do I have to issue refunds to customers in these countries due to this change? A. No. You are not legally responsible for customer refunds for service changes. Q. Do I need to remove these countries from the app distribution list in the Seller Portal? A. No. They will be automatically deleted from March 4, 2024. Additionally, these countries will no longer appear in the Seller Portal distribution list for paid content. Thank you. Galaxy Store Seller Portal Team
